My leadership style is hands-on and data-driven. I don't mandate change — I measure the pain, [[ref-agile-leadership#Data-Driven Decision Making|show the data]], propose a pilot, and let results speak. I apply [[ref-agile-leadership#Servant Leadership|servant leadership]] — removing blockers, not giving orders. I've led from three different positions: as an IC who saw a gap and filled it without being asked, as a Dev Lead who changed how a team worked by volunteering to go first, and as a Technology Manager who owned a roadmap across 25 engineers. In every case, the pattern is the same — [[ls-ownership|take ownership]], [[ls-initiative|show value through action]], and [[ls-transformation|let data settle debates]].

I've also learned that leadership isn't just about your team. At Volvo, I was the [[ls-volvo|sole owner of the entire delivery pipeline]] — coordinating across departments that didn't naturally talk to each other. At Toyota, I [[ls-toyota|stepped into a testing role]] that wasn't my job because the need was there. And I've dealt with [[ls-conflict|internal conflict]] (deployment freeze debate settled with a 6-week data experiment) and [[ls-empathy|external resistance]] (defensive third-party partner turned into a genuine collaborator by going in person).

When things get critical, I stay systematic: [[ls-composure|drove to a Volvo facility at night]] to fix a race condition before they stopped the production line. And I care about the mechanics of good engineering culture — I [[ref-code-review#HOW WE SET IT UP|built code review from scratch]] at KocSistem and [[ls-vision|delivered measurable roadmap results]] across DevOps, security, architecture, and Agile.
