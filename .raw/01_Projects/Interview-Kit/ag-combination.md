# COMBINATION AB — Stepping Up

At Combination, the tech lead and [[ref-agile-ceremonies#Scrum Roles|Product Owner]] were both unavailable during quarter planning. The team had no direction — no priorities set, no scope defined, no commitments to stakeholders.

I stepped in for both roles. Talked to stakeholders directly, went through the [[ref-agile-ceremonies#Sprint Planning|backlog]] with the team, prioritized based on business impact and what was blocking other work. Ran the planning sessions, set realistic goals, and [[ag-adaptability|kept the team aligned through the quarter]].

What I own long-term here goes beyond that one quarter. I've become the person who bridges the gap between engineering and product when needed. I understand both sides — the technical constraints and the business priorities — because I've been a Product Owner before (at KocSistem for the HR platform) and I've been a developer my whole career. When the PO is busy or the tech lead is on vacation, the team doesn't stall because I can fill in.

## Key Experiences
- [[ag-adaptability|OWNERSHIP — stepped in for lead and PO, ran quarter planning, completed all tasks early]]

## Sorulursa

> [!faq]- "Is this something you want to do more of — the PO/lead hybrid role?"
> I like having one foot in each world. Pure management without coding makes me feel disconnected. Pure coding without people impact feels too narrow. The tech lead or senior engineer role where you're technical enough to make architecture decisions but also responsible for team outcomes — that's where I'm most effective.
