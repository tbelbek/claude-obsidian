# Job Interview: "What Are Your Weaknesses?" — Top 5 Golden Responses

Via @gudanglifehack

## 1. "I can be overly detail-oriented"
*(Best for structured / analytical roles)*

"I can spend too much time on details. I've learned to time-box tasks and focus on impact first, then polish only what truly matters."

## 2. "I used to take on too much myself"
*(Great for leadership / growth roles)*

"I used to struggle with delegating because I wanted things done right. I've learned that clear expectations and trust actually produce better results and scale faster."

## 3. "I can be impatient with slow processes"
*(Best for fast-paced companies)*

"I'm naturally fast-moving, so slow processes used to frustrate me. I've learned to channel that into improving systems instead of pushing people."

## 4. "Public speaking didn't come naturally to me"
*(Classic and safe)*

"Public speaking wasn't a strength early on, so I actively practiced it through presentations and feedback. It's improved significantly."

## 5. "I ask a lot of questions early"
*(Very safe for junior / mid roles)*

"Early on, I tend to ask a lot of questions to fully understand expectations. It helps me avoid mistakes later and deliver stronger results."

## Notable Comments from the Thread

> **@namd1nh:** Good weakness answers don't protect your image. They demonstrate your learning loop. Self-awareness is the real competency being tested.

> **@iDenyTheH (hiring manager):** Please don't say #1 in an interview. Hiring managers immediately view it as a cop-out reply.

> **@MrBrissBoss:** They want you to turn a negative into a positive, which is basically asking you to perform a magic trick for a 3% annual raise.

> **@ir0nney:** You can also try flipping the question by talking about a skill you realized you lacked and exactly what steps you took to learn it.

> **@masonmetax:** Just be real. "I'm clumsy, I'm not perfect and I make mistakes all the time. I am continually trying to improve on myself daily." — never failed for me.
