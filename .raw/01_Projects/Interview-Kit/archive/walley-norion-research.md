## Soft Questions for Initial Recruiter Screening

### 1. Autonomy & Decision-Making
> "The job description mentions 'autonomy and influence over tools, methods, and processes.' In practice, how much does the engineering team shape the roadmap versus receiving prioritized features from business? Are we talking about true product team autonomy or more execution autonomy?"

**What to listen for:**
- Do engineers have a seat at the table for roadmap discussions?
- Is there a PM who brings solutions or problems?
- How are technical debt decisions made vs. feature pressure?

### 2. Hybrid Work Reality Check
> "The posting mentions 3 days/week on-site. Is this a flexible guideline or a tracked expectation? How does the team handle situations where someone needs to be remote more occasionally — is there flexibility or is it rigid?"

**What to listen for:**
- Is this enforced or just "encouraged"?
- Do people actually come in 3 days or is it more relaxed?
- How did hybrid work during COVID transition to post-COVID policy?

### 3. On-Call & Work-Life Balance
> "With thousands of daily transactions, I imagine there's some form of on-call rotation. Could you share how the pager duty is structured — rotation frequency, compensation for on-call hours, and how often there are actual after-hours incidents?"

**What to listen for:**
- Rotation schedule (weekly? monthly?)
- Is there extra pay for on-call or just "part of the job"?
- Severity of incidents — occasional alert or constant firefighting?
- Is the team properly staffed or perpetually in reactive mode?

### 4. Career Growth Clarity
> "For a Senior Backend Developer, what does the progression look like? Is there a defined IC (Individual Contributor) track toward Staff/Principal, or is the expectation to eventually move into Tech Lead management roles?"

**What to listen for:**
- Is there a formal career ladder?
- Do Staff+ roles exist at Walley or is it flat?
- What's the typical path for someone senior who wants to keep coding?

### 5. New Code vs. Maintenance Balance
> "The description mentions 'continuous improvement' and 'modern DevOps practices.' In reality, what percentage of time is spent building new features versus maintaining/refactoring existing systems? Is it closer to 50/50 or 80/20?"

**What to listen for:**
- Honest assessment of technical debt burden
- Is there dedicated time for modernization or only "when we have time"?
- Are new systems being built or is it all legacy maintenance?

### 6. Decision Velocity
> "When the team wants to adopt a new technology or architectural pattern — say, a different message queue or a new observability tool — what's the typical timeline? Days, weeks, or months of approval processes?"

**What to listen for:**
- Bureaucracy level
- Is it "ask forgiveness not permission" or committee-driven?
- Who has final say on architectural decisions?

### 7. Team Composition & Collaboration
> "The posting mentions cross-functional collaboration with PMs and designers. Are these roles embedded in the team or shared across multiple teams? What's the designer:developer ratio?"

**What to listen for:**
- Dedicated PM/designer or matrix organization?
- Do you have to "fight" for design resources?
- How integrated is the team really?

## Deep Questions for Technical/Cultural Interviews

### Technical & Engineering Culture

1. **Code Quality Standards**
   - "What does 'quality' mean at Walley? Are there formal code review requirements, coverage thresholds, or is it more informal?"
   - "How do you handle disagreements in code reviews — is it collaborative or gatekeeper-style?"

2. **Technical Debt Strategy**
   - "Is there a dedicated budget/percentage of sprint capacity for technical debt, or is it only addressed when it becomes a blocker?"
   - "Can you give me an example of a major technical debt item the team has recently tackled?"

3. **Testing Culture**
   - "What's the testing philosophy — TDD, BDD, or test-after? What's the current test coverage and is it enforced in CI?"
   - "Are there integration/E2E tests for the checkout flow, or is it mostly unit tests?"

4. **Incident Response**
   - "Walk me through your incident response process. Post-mortems — blameless or otherwise?"
   - "How do you prevent the same incident twice — is there follow-through or is it just documented and forgotten?"

5. **Knowledge Sharing**
   - "How does the team share knowledge? Regular tech talks, documentation culture, pair programming?"
   - "How do you onboard new seniors — sink or swim, or structured mentorship?"

### Culture & Values

6. **Psychological Safety**
   - "Can you give me an example of a time someone challenged a decision and it changed the outcome?"
   - "What happens when someone admits they made a mistake in production?"

7. **Feedback Culture**
   - "How often do you do 1:1s and what do they typically cover — project updates or personal growth?"
   - "Is there a formal feedback mechanism or is it ad-hoc?"

8. **Diversity & Inclusion**
   - "The posting mentions 'bias-free recruitment.' What does that look like in practice beyond not asking for photos?"
   - "What's the current gender/background diversity in the engineering teams?"

9. **Work-Life Balance Reality**
   - "What's a typical week look like — 40 hours or regularly more?"
   - "How do you handle crunch times? Are they exceptional or normalized?"

10. **Remote-First or Office-First Culture**
    - "If I'm remote on a Tuesday and the team is in the office, am I a second-class citizen in meetings or is the culture remote-inclusive?"
    - "Are important decisions made in hallway conversations or documented async?"

### The "Real" Questions (for the end)

11. **Why is the position open?**
    - "What happened to the previous person in this role — growth, departure, new team?"
    - "How long has this role been open and why?"

12. **The Team's Pain Points**
    - "What's the biggest challenge the team is facing right now?"
    - "What would success look like for this role in the first 6 months?"

13. **Red Flags to Probe**
    - "What do you wish you knew before you joined Walley?"
    - "What's something that frustrates you about working here?"

## Red Flags to Watch For

| Flag | Question to Ask | Bad Answer Indicators |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **On-call burnout** | "How often do people get paged after hours?" | "Oh, it's not that bad" (vague), no rotation clarity |
| **Feature factory** | "How much time is spent on tech debt vs features?" | "We focus on delivering value" (no numbers), "when we have time" |
| **Bureaucracy** | "How long to adopt a new tool?" | "We have a process for that" (implying months), committee approvals |
| **No growth path** | "What's the IC track look like?" | "You could become a Tech Lead" (only management path), no Staff/Principal roles |
| **Hybrid theater** | "Is 3 days/week flexible?" | "It's mandatory" with no exceptions, tracked badge swipes |
| **Legacy trap** | "What's the new code vs maintenance ratio?" | "We're always firefighting" or "it's 90% maintenance" |
| **No psychological safety** | "Tell me about a time someone challenged a decision" | "We trust our leaders" (no examples), blame-shifting stories |

## Notes from Research

**Checkout Team Pros:**
- Modern tech stack (Terraform, Kubernetes, modern .NET)
- High-impact product (millions of users)
- English-speaking team
- Greenfield development focus
- True DevOps ownership

**Checkout Team Cons:**
- 3 days/week on-site requirement
- High-transaction system = potential on-call stress
- Financial services = compliance overhead
- Norion Bank Group structure = potential corporate bureaucracy

**Core Team Specifics:**
- Legacy stack (WPF, NHibernate, SOAP) is a significant concern
- "Support/drift/underhåll" responsibilities suggest operational burden
- Swedish language requirement is a blocker for non-Swedish speakers
- Two open positions suggests either growth or turnover

**Overall Assessment:**
Checkout role aligns well with current skillset (.NET 8, Azure from Eliq application) and career goals. Modern stack and product focus are strong positives. Core role is less attractive due to legacy stack and language requirements.
